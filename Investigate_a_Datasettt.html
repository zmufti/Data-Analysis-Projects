NCIS Background Checks¶

    Since November, 1998, the FBI has required by the Brady Handgun Violence Prevention Act of 1993 that Federal Firearm Licensees run background checks in order to determine eligibility of prospective buyers to purchase firearms or explosives. Cashiers at firearm vendors must initiate checks with the FBI determining that their customers do not have criminal records before a sale is completed. Since the implementation of this system (the National Instant Criminal Background Check System or NCIS) researchers have used the number of background checks as a proxy for total gun purchases during a given period of time. Although a one-to-one correlation between a gun background check and a sale cannot be made, using background checks is still the most widely accepted method for firearm sales estimation.

In this report, we will look at United States data released by the FBI with the numbers of differing types of firearm background checks made for each state by month and year since 1998. We will use Python to first investigate this data and estimate gun sales; then, we will compare it with Census data gathered from 2010-2016.

Yearly Estimated Sales Totals¶

In the code cell below, we have a function, year_totals(), which will give us the yearly totals for a given column in a specified DataFrame for a specified number of years.

In [889]:
def year_totals(df, column_name, years):
    """Takes a df, column name, and year and gives yearly totals of a selected column in the form of a dictionary"""
    yearly_totals = {}
    for year in years:
        yearly_totals[year] = int(sum(df.loc[df['year'] == year, column_name]))  
    return yearly_totals

def state_year_totals(df, column_name, state, years):
    """This function returns a dictionary containing state yearly totals 
       for a given column of a specified DataFrame"""
    st_yearly_totals = {}
    state_df = df.loc[state, ['year', column_name]]
    for year in years:
        st_yearly_totals[year] = sum(state_df.loc[state_df['year'] == year, column_name]) 
    return st_yearly_totals

Guns and Population Density¶

    Given that the we are using estimations of gun sales, our conclusions this far cannot be 100% accurate. However, given the data we have, we can begin to make the case that not only have gun numbers increased in over the past decade, but also that gun popularity has increased due to a decreasing average proportion of people to guns sold across the United States. There may be also a relationship between gun popularity and population density, as many states in our data set that are more urban (dense population) have lower gun popularity (people per gun), while many rural states (sparse population) have higher gun popularity. These results may make sense for 2010, but to establish that these conclusions are true in general, we would need Census and background check data for many more years.

Research Limitations¶

Throughout this analysis, there were many limitations that could have led to less than accurate results:

1) Background Checks are not correlated 1-to-1 with gun sales; meaning that we cannot definitively say that gun sales are going up when background check levels are going up. Therefore, the background check data is not sufficient to prove our results; we would need data on actual sales to do so.

2)Furthermore, the data was not 100% complete; for example, we ommitted Hawaii at one point in the analysis because it lacked data. We then computed averages to summarize the U.S. when there was actually a state missing (1 state could provide enough data to change results significantly in a set of 50 states).

3) State laws differ from state to state, and some states have different ways of measuring data. So, when we compared state levels, many of the results attributed to one variable (such as population density) could have instead been the results of differences in state laws.

Conclusions¶

In this analysis, we have learned quite a bit about guns in the United States in recent history, even if we are using backgroud checks as a proxy for sales.

1) The amount of guns purchased have probably increased year to year (if background checks somewhat accurately estimate the numbers, they surely are).

2) Increases in population may not be the sole cause of the fact that more people are buying guns as time goes on, as an increasing percentage of the population has been getting background checked (buying guns) in recent years.

3) Guns may be more popular in rural regions and less popular in urban regions (although state laws might have something to do with this).
